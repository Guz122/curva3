Borsch

Borsch is a Ukrainian liquid dish made from chopped beets, cabbage with potatoes and various seasonings. It is a collective name for various variants of the first dish, the common feature of which is probably a sour, sour taste. Most often, the word "borsch" refers to red borsch. It is the main first dish of Ukrainian cuisine. It has gained international recognition. Borsch is a favorite dish of Ukrainians, a symbol of Ukrainian culinary culture and prosperity(Back to Top)

Deruny

Deruny - potato pancakes, are very popular in Ukrainian, Polish, Belarusian, Czech, Slovak, Bulgarian, Austrian and German cuisines, in Ukraine they are especially widespread in the northern and western regions. The main ingredient of the dish is grated potatoes.(Back to Top)

Nalysnyky

Nalysnyky are thin pancakes with a filling, most often with cottage cheese. A similar source of the word "nalysnyk" is the phrase "on a leaf", that is, it was a cake baked on a large cabbage leaf or on a baking sheet.Pancakes for nalysnyky are made of unleavened egg dough on a hot frying pan greased with fat. Fried pancakes are stuffed and wrapped or folded. Nalysnyky are filled with cottage cheese, jam, mushrooms, meat, berries, raisins, boiled cereals, fish caviar, etc. Often pancakes are lightly re-fried, saut√©ed or baked.
